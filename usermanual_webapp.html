Overview

The p-Chip Cloud web application is a cloud hosted platform where clients can set-up and manage their organization and access the historical information of all of their products using p-Chip microtransponder technology across the supply chain.

Getting Started

This guide will help you as your account administrator get up and running quickly to start experiencing the incredible benefits that securing your products with p-Chip microtransponder technology offers to you and your customers

To read more about the benefits of p-Chip security start your journey here.

Prerequisites

  • Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge or other compatible web browser
  • Microsoft Office Account

Requesting an Account

  1. In your web browser, go to http://pchip-web-portal.azurewebsites.net/
  2. Select Request Account
  3. Fill in the form with your companies information
  4. Submit the form by selecting the Request Account button when complete

Logging In

  1. In your web browser, go to http://pchip-web-portal.azurewebsites.net/
  2. Select Sign in With Office 365
  3. Allow the p-Chip Cloud to read your Microsoft Profile by selecting Yes when prompted

Settings

The application settings is where you can control your account information. This information is required for security purposes. This information can be changed by your company's administrator at any time.

Components

  1. Organization Name: The registered name of your business or organization
  2. Email: Company email
  3. Telephone: Company telephone number
  4. Industry: Company industry
  5. Street: Street: Registered street address
  6. City: City: Registered city
  7. Post Code: Registered postal code
  8. Country: Registered country
  9. Service Agreements: Terms of service for use of the application

User Interface Guide

Homepage

Once logged in you will be presented with your organization's homepage. This is the core of the application from which you can manage your organization's supply chain tracking with p-Chip Cloud. You can navigate through the application using the tabs on the left hand side of the homepage.

Components

  1. Dashboard: The dashboard shows you an overview of your organization's products tags with p-Chip. You can view the last scanned location and view the historical global activity of your products tagged with p-Chip microtransponder tags
  2. Users: Web application users
  3. Licenses: p-Chip Cloud Desktop Application licenses issued by your organization
  4. Groups: License groups
  5. Products: Products that can be associated with p-Chip microtransponder tags
  6. Tests: Define tests for products and record the results in the cloud
  7. Tags: Your organizations products using p-Chip microtransponder tags
  8. Tag Activity: Historical information of all scans performed by organization licenses
  9. Error Reports: Issues and error reporting from p-Chip Cloud Desktop Application users.
  10. Settings: Organization details
  11. Tracked Items: Total number of p-Chip microtransponder tags used by your organization
  12. Active Items: Number of p-Chip microtransponder tags with an Active status
  13. In-Active Items: Number of p-Chip microtransponder tags with an In-Active status
  14. Activity: Total number of Activities or scans by p-Chip Cloud desktop application users
  15. Tracked Items: (Map) The last scanned location for your organization's items tagged with p-Chip microtransponder technology visualized by location
  16. Tracking Activity: (Chart) Time series chart showing the overall Activities performed by p-Chip Cloud Desktop App users across your organization

Dashboard

The p-Chip Cloud Dashboard is the command center for managing your global supply chain. It is designed to give you a high level understanding of where your products are, the overall activity of your supply chain and the number or percentage of goods lost or Inactive.

Components

  1. Tracked Items: Total number of p-Chips applied and recorded by your organization in the p-Chip Cloud tracking system.
  2. Active Items: Total number of p-Chips with a status of Active
  3. Inactive Items: Total number of p-Chips with a status of Inactive
  4. Activity: Total number of scans of p-Chips performed by users with active licenses across your organization.
  5. Tracked Items (Map): Last scanned location of p-Chip microtransponder tags created by your organization.
  6. Tracking Activity (Bar Chart): Total number of scans of p-Chips performed by users with active licenses across your organization's plotted over time.

Licenses

Licenses are used by the p-Chip Cloud Desktop Application to authorize your organization's devices to create and search for your products with p-Chip microtransponder tags. Licenses are issued on a device basis meaning that they cannot be reused across devices. Licenses are issued by the client administrator through the web portal. Licenses that have been issued to your organization and are no longer in use can be deactivated at any time.

Components

  1. Your organization List of Licenses are accessible via the main navigation menu

Groups

Groups are how data is segregated with an organization. Groups can be a physical location, a department or any other type of a group of devices. Groups allow p-Chip Cloud Desktop App users to share data, collaborate and stay up to date with team activities no matter where they are in the world. Groups reside within an organization and cannot span outside of the organization.

The p-Chip status, a value of Active or Inactive, is used to control the tracking status of the item. If an item is lost, stolen or is not fit for use or sale, the tag can be deactivated which notifies the manufacturing and supply chain that the attached item is no longer authenticated by your organization. To deactivate a tag:

  1. Select a Tag from the Tag List

  3. Select to return to the Tag List

Tag Activities

Tag activities are specific events that are recorded in the p-Chip Cloud and made accessible across your organization. There are currently 4 activities currently supported. These include:

  • A tag is added to your organization
  • A product is added to an existing tag in your organization. A single p-Chip can be "converted" into multiple products during its life cycle, just as goods may change from raw materials to intermediates to finished goods. Each transformation of a good is captured with the Product Add event.
  • A test is performed on a tag.
  • A tag is searched by a user within your organization.

During each of these activities, metadata on time, location, device, equipment and user information is collected and stored to improve visibility across your global supply chain.

Components

  1. Your organization List of Tag Activities are accessible via the main navigation menu
  2. Find specific items quickly by filtering on:
    • - p-Chip serial number
    • - External ID
    • - Product
    • - License
    • - Group
    • - Type
  3. Export a full list of your organization's Tag Activities in csv format
  4. View a full list of Tag Activities created by your organization

Error Reports

Error reports are user generated reports across your organization. These reports are created in the p-Chp Cloud Desktop App and are designed to allow quick and easy communication between your organization administrators and field users. Here users can report equipment issues, product issues and more.
